CC Theory - Intro
\[ | \Psi_{\text{CC}} \rangle = e^T | \Psi_0 \rangle \]
\[ T_1 = \sum_{i = 1}^{N_{\text{occ}}} \sum_{a = 1}^{N_{\text{vir}}} t_i^a a_a^\dagger a_i,\] \[\quad T_2 = \frac{1}{4} \sum_{i, j = 1}^{N_{\text{occ}}} \sum_{a, b = 1}^{N_{\text{vir}}} t_{ij}^{ab} a_a^\dagger a_b^\dagger a_j a_i, ... \]
\[ \begin{aligned} \text{CC energy equation}\quad & E_{\text{CC}} = \langle \Psi_0 | e^{-T} H e^T | \Psi_0 \rangle \\[6pt] \text{Residual equations}\quad & R_{\mu} = \langle \Psi_{\mu} | e^{-T} H e^T | \Psi_0 \rangle = 0 \end{aligned} \]

Fixed-Point (FP) Iteration Transformation
\[ \text{MO[4]} \quad t^{ab}_{ij} = \frac{v^{ab}_{ij} + \Delta f^{ab}_{ij}(t) + \bar{R}^{ab}_{ij}(t)}{f^{i}_{i} + f^{j}_{j} - f^{a}_{a} - f^{b}_{b}} \]
\[ \text{AR[1]} \quad \theta^{\lambda\sigma}_{\mu\nu} = \frac{Y^{\lambda\sigma}_{\mu\nu} + \Delta F^{\lambda\sigma}_{\mu\nu}(\theta) + R^{\lambda\sigma}_{\mu\nu}(\theta)}{{}^{o}F^{\mu}_\mu + {}^{o}F^{\nu}_\nu - {}^{v}F^{\lambda}_\lambda - {}^{v}F^{\sigma}_\sigma} \]

Backup - CC theory in Arbitrary Gauge
Idea: We use a unitary matrix for the basis transformation.
\[ \boxed{ \begin{array}{c} \text{CC theory} \\ \text{(canonical basis)} \end{array} } \;\xrightarrow{\;\text{Basis transformation } (U)\;}\; \boxed{ \begin{array}{c} \text{CC theory} \\ \text{(arbitrary basis)} \end{array} } \]
AR basis is not, in general, orthogonal. To ensure orthogonality, we define,
\[ \bar{U}_{\mu i} = S_{\mu}^{\alpha} U_{\alpha i}, \]
where "$\mu$" is an AO index and "$S$" denotes the overlap matrix in the AO basis which has the property,
\[ U_{\mu p} \bar{U}_{\mu q} = U_{\mu p} S_{\mu}^{\alpha} U_{\alpha q} = \delta_{pq}. \]
Backup - Projectors
The integral transformation procedure contains 2 steps:
\[ \boxed{ \begin{array}{c} \text{AO} \\ \text{Integrals} \end{array} } \;\xrightarrow{\;C\;}\; \boxed{ \begin{array}{c} \text{MO} \\ \text{Integrals} \end{array} } \;\xrightarrow{\;U\;}\; \boxed{ \begin{array}{c} \text{AR} \\ \text{Integrals} \end{array} } \]
The projectors onto the occupied and virtual subspace take the form,
\[ P_{\mu}^{\nu} = C_{\mu i} U_{\nu i}, \quad \quad \quad Q_{\mu}^{\nu} = U_{\mu a} C_{\nu a}. \]
together with their overlap-weighted counterparts
\[ \bar{P}_{\mu}^{\nu} = \bar{U}_{\mu i} C_{\nu i}, \quad \quad \quad \bar{Q}_{\lambda}^{\sigma} = \bar{U}_{\lambda a} C_{\sigma a}. \]
Backup - Elements in AR basis
We now derive the transformed electron repulsion integrals as,
\[ \pi_{\mu\nu}^{\lambda\sigma} = P_{\mu\alpha} P_{\nu\beta} \nu_{\alpha\beta}^{\gamma\delta} Q_{\gamma\lambda} Q_{\delta\sigma}. \]
The corresponding transformed amplitudesas as,
\[ \theta_{\mu\nu}^{\lambda\sigma} = \bar{U}_{\mu i} \bar{U}_{\nu j} \bar{U}_{\lambda a} \bar{U}_{\sigma b} t_{ij}^{ab}. \]
With these we can transform CC equation from MO basis to AR basis as,
\[ Z_{\mu\nu}^{\lambda\sigma} (\theta) = \bar{U}_{\mu i} \bar{U}_{\nu j} \bar{U}_{\lambda a} \bar{U}_{\sigma b} \, Z_{ij}^{ab} (t) = 0. \]
