Introduction

Coupled-Cluster (CC) theory is a cornerstone of high-accuracy electronic structure methods. Convergence of fixed-point iterations remains a critical numerical challenge.

Methodology

We analyze the fixed-point map in both MO and AO representations.

$$ t^{(k+1)} = \frac{R(t^{(k)})}{D} $$

Spectral radius of the Jacobian is used as a stability metric.

Results

  • Convergence strongly depends on denominator shifts.
  • AO formulation shows improved robustness.
  • Spectral radius correlates with iteration count.

Discussion

Shifting the denominator stabilizes near-singular updates and improves convergence domains.

Conclusions

  • Fixed-point robustness is basis dependent.
  • Denominator regularization is essential.
  • Future work: adaptive shift strategies.
